Learn More About Each Type

Phishing

Phishing scams are attempts by fraudsters to obtain sensitive information such as usernames, passwords, and credit card details by disguising as a trustworthy entity in electronic communications.

  • Be cautious of unsolicited emails that request personal information.
  • Check the email address of the sender carefully.
  • Avoid clicking on suspicious links or downloading attachments from unknown sources.

Identity Theft

Identity theft occurs when someone uses your personal information without your permission to commit fraud or other crimes. It can have serious repercussions on your finances and credit history.

  • Use strong, unique passwords for your accounts.
  • Monitor your credit report regularly for any suspicious activity.
  • Be cautious when sharing personal information online.

Investment Scams

Investment scams involve offers of high returns with little or no risk. These scams can take many forms, including Ponzi schemes, pump and dump schemes, and advance fee fraud.

  • Be skeptical of investments that promise guaranteed returns.
  • Research the investment and the company behind it thoroughly.
  • Consult a financial advisor before making large investments.

Online Shopping Fraud

Online shopping fraud involves fake websites or sellers that trick consumers into paying for goods that never arrive. These scams can also involve counterfeit products being sold as genuine.

  • Shop from reputable and well-known websites.
  • Read reviews and ratings of sellers before making a purchase.
  • Use secure payment methods like credit cards or PayPal.
